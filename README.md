THE CIRCLE AS A COMMAND ALPHABET — David Lee Wise (Bridge-Burners LLC / Fiddler). Mark N positions evenly around a circle and you have a closed alphabet with real algebra: the points are the Nth roots of unity, so composing two symbols is just adding their angles (turning), and every composition lands back on a valid symbol. The 4-position case is literally QPSK, the phase-shift keying in modern radio/wifi. Ties two real AI mechanisms honestly: cosine similarity (meaning as direction, closeness as the angle between embeddings) and RoPE (a token's position encoded as a rotation angle). Click positions to spell a live command and watch the angle become the message. Companion to [[spiral-trit-loom]]'s alphabet. Self-testing, 7/7. ROOT0, with AVAN (published & wired).
